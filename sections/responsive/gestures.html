Intuitive Gesture Design

Touch interactions should feel natural, discoverable, and enhance the user experience

DO

  • ✓ Use standard gestures consistently across platforms
  • ✓ Provide visual cues and affordances for available gestures
  • ✓ Ensure touch targets are at least 44×44 points (iOS) or 48×48dp (Android)
  • ✓ Add sufficient space between interactive elements (min 8px)
  • ✓ Provide immediate visual feedback for touch interactions
  • ✓ Test gestures with real users on actual devices
  • ✓ Consider edge cases, accessibility, and motor impairments
  • ✓ Provide alternative interaction methods (buttons, voice, etc.)

DON'T

  • ✗ Invent new gestures without clear need or onboarding
  • ✗ Hide essential functions behind undiscoverable gestures
  • ✗ Create gesture conflicts that trigger multiple actions
  • ✗ Make touch targets too small or position them too close together
  • ✗ Require precise gestures that are difficult to perform
  • ✗ Ignore platform conventions (iOS vs Android gestures)
  • ✗ Rely solely on gestures without visible UI alternatives
  • ✗ Expect users to discover complex gesture combinations

Common Touch Gestures

Tap

Quick touch and release to select or activate an item

Buttons Links Menu items

Long Press

Touch and hold for 500ms+ to reveal additional options

Context menus Selection mode Peek & pop

Double-Tap

Two quick taps in rapid succession

Zoom to fit Like content Quick actions

Swipe

Quick horizontal or vertical sliding motion

Navigation Reveal actions Dismiss items

Pinch

Two fingers moving together or apart

Zoom in/out Scale content Expand maps

Rotation

Two fingers moving in a circular motion

Rotate images Adjust dials Change orientation

Gesture Design Principles

Discoverability

Users should be able to easily discover what gestures are available through visual cues and animations.

Consistency

Use gestures consistently throughout your application and follow platform conventions.

Feedback

Provide immediate visual or haptic feedback when a gesture is recognized or completed.

Forgiveness

Design gestures to be forgiving of small errors and allow users to cancel or undo actions.
