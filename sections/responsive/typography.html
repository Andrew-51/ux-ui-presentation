Good vs. Poor Typography

DO

Clear Heading with Proper Weight

Body text is legible with sufficient line height and proper spacing. The contrast between text and background is strong, making content easy to read even for extended periods.

Secondary information maintains readable contrast while visually distinct from primary content.

Posted on May 12 5 min read
  • Font size: 16px minimum for body text
  • Line height: 1.5× for good readability
  • Contrast ratio: At least 4.5:1 for normal text
  • Clear hierarchy: Distinct visual levels
DON'T

hard to read heading with poor capitalization

Body text that's too small with insufficient line height. This creates difficulty when reading longer passages of text, especially on smaller screens or for users with visual impairments. The lack of proper paragraph spacing further compounds readability issues.

Nearly invisible secondary text that strains the eyes and becomes completely unreadable in certain lighting conditions or for users with slight visual impairments.

Posted 12/5 5m read
  • Font size: Too small (< 12px)
  • Line height: Insufficient (< 1.2×)
  • Contrast ratio: Too low (< 3:1)
  • Poor hierarchy: Confusing visual weight
