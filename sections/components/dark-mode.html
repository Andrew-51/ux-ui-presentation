Effective Dark Mode Design

Dark mode is not just inverting colors

DO

Dark Mode Done Right

Primary text remains highly readable with 87% opacity.

Secondary text at 60% opacity maintains the hierarchy.

Uses varying opacities and subtle surfaces

✓ Use dark gray instead of pure black
✓ Reduce contrast for comfortable reading
✓ Maintain clear text hierarchy
DON'T

Poor Dark Mode

Text with 100% white creates eye strain.

Secondary text with poor contrast ratio.

Uses pure black with harsh contrasts

✗ Pure black causes eye strain
✗ 100% white text is too harsh
✗ Poor color choices decrease readability

Dark Mode Design Guidelines

→

Background Colors

Use dark grays (#121212 to #2C2C2E) instead of pure black, and reduce white to 87-95% opacity

Text Hierarchy

Primary: 87% white, Secondary: 60% white, Disabled: 38% white

Elevation & Shadows

Use white overlays at 5-16% to indicate elevation instead of shadows

Accent Colors

Use lighter, less saturated colors (increase lightness by 15-30%)
